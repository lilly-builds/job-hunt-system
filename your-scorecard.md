# Your Scorecard — what makes a role worth your time

This is the heart of the system. Fill it in honestly, then the **Aligned Roles** page (and Claude) scores every role against it instead of against generic advice. Replace the prompts below with your real answers. Delete the examples.

> Tip: open this folder in Claude Code and say *"interview me to fill in my scorecard."* It'll ask the questions and write your answers here.

---

## 1. The role you want
The titles / shape of role you're actually going after.

- The shape that matters to you: *(people to lead? execs to convince? hands-on building? change/adoption?)*

## 4. Company profile  *(what kind of company is the right forge)*
- Stage / size you want: _______
- Industries you'd love / avoid: _______
- A "tailwind, not a sinking ship" check: *is this market growing or structurally declining?*

## 5. How to score  *(instructions for Claude)*
When grading a role against this scorecard:
1. **Fail fast:** if any non-negotiable in section 2 clearly fails, mark it ✗ and stop — note which one.
2. Otherwise score **1–10** on overall fit (role shape + company profile + preferences).
3. Write a one-line **"Why you"** that names the specific match.
4. Add an honest **caveat** if comp/location/role-shape is uncertain — never oversell.
5. Render it as a card in `aligned-roles.html` (copy the example card format).

---
*The point of a written scorecard: when you're tempted to talk a standard down to make an offer fit — that's the trap, not the standard. Re-read this instead of lowering it.*
